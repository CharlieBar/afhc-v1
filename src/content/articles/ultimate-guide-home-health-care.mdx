## What Is Home Health Care?

Home health care is skilled medical care provided in a patient's home by licensed healthcare professionals — registered nurses, physical therapists, occupational therapists, speech-language pathologists, and home health aides. It is ordered by a physician and, for most patients, covered by Medicare with no out-of-pocket cost.

This is different from "home care" or "personal care" services like companionship, cooking, or housekeeping. Home health care is clinical — it requires the same knowledge and skill as care provided in a hospital or clinic, delivered in the most comfortable setting possible: your home.

## Who Needs Home Health Care?

Home health care is appropriate for people across a wide range of situations:

**After a hospital discharge:** Most people who receive home health care are transitioning from a hospital or rehabilitation facility. A nurse begins visiting within 24–48 hours to prevent complications and reduce the risk of readmission.

**Managing a chronic condition:** Heart failure, COPD, diabetes, Parkinson's disease, and other chronic conditions often require regular skilled monitoring, medication management, and education to stay stable at home.

**Following surgery:** After joint replacement, cardiac surgery, abdominal surgery, or any major procedure, professional nursing and therapy at home accelerates recovery and prevents wound complications.

**After a stroke or neurological event:** Stroke survivors often need skilled nursing combined with physical, occupational, and speech therapy — all of which can be performed at home.

**Declining at home:** Seniors who are losing function, experiencing falls, or showing signs of cognitive decline often benefit significantly from skilled assessment and targeted therapy.

## What Services Are Included in Home Health Care?

A Medicare-certified home health agency like All Family Health Care provides the full spectrum of skilled services:

**Skilled Nursing:** Registered nurses provide wound care, medication management, IV therapy, vital signs monitoring, health teaching, and complex condition management. A skilled nurse brings the clinical judgment of a hospital nurse directly to your home.

**Physical Therapy:** Licensed physical therapists develop personalized exercise programs to rebuild strength, restore balance, improve gait, and reduce fall risk — performed in the actual rooms and spaces where patients live.

**Occupational Therapy:** OTs help patients relearn daily activities — bathing, dressing, cooking, grooming — safely and independently. They also assess the home for hazards and recommend adaptive equipment.

**Speech-Language Pathology:** Speech therapists treat disorders of communication, voice, cognition, and swallowing — especially important for stroke survivors, Parkinson's patients, and those with head/neck conditions.

**Home Health Aide:** Aides provide personal care assistance — bathing, grooming, hygiene — when prescribed as part of a skilled care plan. They provide critical support for patients who need help with activities of daily living.

**Medical Social Services:** Licensed social workers help patients and families navigate resources, plan for long-term care needs, and address the emotional challenges of illness.

## Does Medicare Cover Home Health Care?

Yes — and this surprises many families. Medicare Part A and Part B cover home health care when the following conditions are met:

1. **Your doctor orders the care** — A physician, nurse practitioner, or physician assistant must certify that you need skilled care and sign your care plan.

2. **You are homebound** — Medicare defines homebound as requiring considerable effort to leave your home due to illness, injury, or disability. You can still leave for medical appointments or other essential activities.

3. **You need skilled care** — You must need skilled nursing or therapy services (not just personal care assistance).

4. **The agency is Medicare-certified** — The home health agency must be certified by Medicare and meet all CMS standards.

When these criteria are met, Medicare covers 100% of approved home health visits — no copays, no deductibles.

## How to Choose a Home Health Agency in Chicago

Not all home health agencies are equal. Here's what to look for:

**Medicare Certification:** Only use a Medicare-certified agency. This certification requires meeting rigorous federal quality and safety standards.

**Joint Commission Accreditation:** The Gold Seal of Approval from The Joint Commission is the highest accreditation in healthcare. It means the agency meets the same standards as top hospitals. Not all agencies have this — ask specifically.

**Consistency of Care:** Ask whether you'll have the same nurse and therapists throughout your care. Consistent caregivers build trust and are better at detecting subtle changes in condition.

**Local and Established:** A local agency with deep roots in your community is more accountable and more responsive than a national chain.

**Communication:** A good agency communicates with your physician regularly and keeps your family informed with progress updates.
